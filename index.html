OpenQual

An open standard for the credentials, skills, and demonstrated competencies responders earn across a career.

The problem

Qualification data in emergency services is fragmented. A responder's career spans jobs in the fire service, gigs in private ambulances, and volunteering on ski patrols, wildfire mitigation ops, and SAR missions. A paramedic's certifications live duplicated across incompatible digital records systems, while their fire taskbook completions live on paper. Training records from one department are stranded when the responder moves to another.

None of this data travels with the responder when they move between departments or disciplines, because it lives in unbreakable silos, and none of it belongs to the responder who earned it.

There's no common language for what a certification record looks like, what a completed taskbook is, or how readiness is calculated — whether you're qualifying a firefighter to go interior, an EMT to start an IV, or a recruit to finish probation.

What OpenQual is

OpenQual is an open data standard of schemas, data models, and calculation tools that define how qualification data is structured and exchanged. It covers the full arc of a responder's qualifications: individual credentials and certifications, structured taskbooks and skillsheets, and the renewal progress that keeps those credentials current across a career.

The standard is free, open, and owned by no single vendor. Any department can adopt it. Any developer can build on it. Any system can implement it.

What OpenQual isn't

OpenQual is not an application. It's the foundation applications are built on. It defines the language, not the system.

Built by those who do the work and those who want to make our work a little easier.
